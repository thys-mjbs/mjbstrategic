Profit Concentration Analyzer

Profit concentration measures how much of a company’s total profit is generated by its most important revenue segments rather than simply looking at total sales. Businesses often appear diversified when viewed through revenue alone, yet the underlying profit engine may rely heavily on a small number of activities.

In operational terms this issue commonly arises across product lines, service categories, or major customer groups. A business may generate revenue from many sources while the majority of profit is produced by only one or two segments. When this occurs the business becomes structurally dependent on those activities.

Understanding where profit is actually generated helps operators identify strategic risk, operational leverage points, and areas where disruption could materially affect the company’s financial stability.
