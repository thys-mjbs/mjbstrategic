Profit Concentration Analyzer

Diagnostic Introduction

Businesses often review total revenue when evaluating performance, yet revenue alone does not reveal where profit is actually generated. Two segments with identical revenue may contribute very different levels of profit depending on margin structure.

This diagnostic estimates how profit is distributed across different revenue segments such as product lines, service categories, or major customer groups. By calculating the profit contribution from each segment, the tool highlights whether the business depends on a narrow set of activities to generate most of its margin.

In operational environments this issue commonly appears when one or two offerings quietly generate the majority of earnings while other activities consume resources but contribute little profit. Identifying profit concentration allows operators to evaluate structural risk and understand which activities truly sustain the business.
