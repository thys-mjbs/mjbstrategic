Profit Concentration Analyzer

Profit concentration measures how much of a company’s total profit is generated by a small number of revenue segments. Revenue alone does not reveal where economic value is created inside the business. Two segments may produce similar revenue while generating very different profit outcomes due to differences in pricing, cost structure, or operational efficiency.

Operators frequently encounter this issue when reviewing product lines, service categories, or customer groups that appear important based on sales volume but contribute less profit than expected. Understanding which activities generate the majority of profit helps management allocate resources, manage operational risk, and identify areas where margin performance drives the economic engine of the business.
