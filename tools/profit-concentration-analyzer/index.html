Profit Concentration Analyzer

This diagnostic measures how much of your total gross profit comes from your top revenue segments. It converts segment revenue and gross margin into estimated profit contribution. It then calculates what share of profit is generated by the top segment and the top two combined.

Operationally this shows up when a business has many orders but only a few product lines or customer groups actually generate margin. Pricing, discounting, and cost structure differences across segments can create hidden profit dependency. A segment can look “small” in revenue but dominate profit because margin is higher or costs are better controlled.

This matters because management decisions should protect and strengthen the real profit engine, not just chase topline growth. Concentrated profit increases vulnerability if pricing shifts, a key customer leaves, or supplier terms tighten. Diversified profit improves resilience and gives operators more options for capital allocation and planning.

Many of the inputs requested by this diagnostic are metrics that disciplined operators typically monitor through internal management reporting. If a number requested here is not immediately available it often indicates that the current reporting structure does not isolate that metric clearly. Businesses operating with strong financial visibility normally track these figures regularly because they influence pricing decisions, supplier negotiations, operational planning, capital allocation, and risk management. Part of the work performed by MJB Strategic often involves helping companies design internal reporting structures that surface these metrics consistently so management can make better operational decisions.
