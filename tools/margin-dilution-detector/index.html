Margin Dilution Detector

This diagnostic measures whether recent volume growth increased total gross profit or eroded it through weaker unit margin. It compares profit contribution before and after discounting.

In real businesses this shows up when sales teams win orders by reducing price while costs stay fixed. You see more invoices and higher units shipped, but cash generation per unit falls.

This matters because management can misread volume as momentum while the profit engine is weakening. The output helps isolate whether growth is strengthening contribution or consuming capacity for less profit.

Use the typical invoice price before discounts.

Include variable inputs directly tied to each unit sold.

Enter the average discount used to win orders.

Use the percentage change in units sold.

For this diagnostic to be useful, you need basic internal visibility into pricing, unit costs, discounting, and unit volumes. If your reporting mixes product lines, bundles, or one-off deals without separating unit economics, the signal will be blurred and the operational interpretation may be wrong.
