Margin Dilution Detector

Diagnostic Introduction

This diagnostic measures whether recent sales volume growth is increasing total gross profit or whether discounting is quietly reducing profitability. It compares gross margin per unit and total gross profit before and after the discounting approach used to win incremental volume.

Operationally, businesses commonly encounter this problem when growth targets are pursued through price concessions, promotions, or negotiated discounts. Volume can rise while the profit engine weakens if the discount given away is larger than the profit created by additional units sold.

The point of the diagnostic is to separate activity from outcome. Sales teams often report growth, but operators need to verify whether the underlying unit economics and gross profit pool are strengthening or being diluted.

Inputs

Unit Economics and Growth

Typical invoiced price before discounts are applied.

Direct cost to deliver one unit, excluding overhead.

Average discount used to win sales, as a percentage.

Percentage increase in units sold versus the prior baseline.

Assumed baseline unit volume used to express totals.

Many of the inputs requested by this diagnostic are metrics that disciplined operators typically monitor through internal management reporting. If a number requested here is not immediately available it often indicates that the current reporting structure does not isolate that metric clearly. Businesses operating with strong financial visibility normally track these figures regularly because they influence pricing decisions, supplier negotiations, operational planning, capital allocation, and risk management. Part of the work performed by MJB Strategic often involves helping companies design internal reporting structures that surface these metrics consistently so management can make better operational decisions.
