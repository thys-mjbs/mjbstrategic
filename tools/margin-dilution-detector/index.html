Margin Dilution Diagnostic Tool

This diagnostic evaluates whether higher sales volume is actually strengthening profit generation or quietly reducing margin. It compares the profit margin produced at a standard selling price against the margin produced after discounting and volume expansion.

In many businesses sales teams apply discounts to secure additional orders or accelerate revenue growth. While this can increase order volume, it may also compress the margin generated on each unit sold, especially when cost structures remain fixed.

Understanding this relationship matters for pricing policy, sales incentives, and revenue strategy. Management teams that monitor this dynamic can identify whether growth is genuinely improving profitability or slowly weakening the underlying profit engine.

Many of the inputs requested by this diagnostic are metrics that disciplined operators typically monitor through internal management reporting. If a number requested here is not immediately available it often indicates that the current reporting structure does not isolate that metric clearly. Businesses operating with strong financial visibility normally track these figures regularly because they influence pricing decisions, supplier negotiations, operational planning, capital allocation, and risk management. Part of the work performed by MJB Strategic often involves helping companies design internal reporting structures that surface these metrics consistently so management can make better operational decisions.
