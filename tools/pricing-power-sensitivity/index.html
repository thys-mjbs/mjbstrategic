Pricing Power Sensitivity Calculator

This diagnostic estimates how total profit shifts when prices rise by small increments under your assumed demand response. It treats gross margin as a stable percentage and tests whether profit improves without adding volume.

In real businesses, price moves show up as list price changes, discount tightening, surcharge policies, or minimum order enforcement. The operational reality is that demand rarely stays flat, so the net outcome depends on how orders, churn, and deal sizes react.

This matters because small price decisions compound across every invoice and can outperform growth efforts if demand holds. It also exposes when the business is already at the edge of customer tolerance and needs a tighter pricing and retention plan.

Use last 12 months billed revenue, before tax.

Use gross margin after direct costs and COGS.

Negative means fewer orders; positive means demand increases.

This tool assumes your internal reporting can separate price, volume, and margin by product, customer, and channel. If you cannot track discounts, churn, and order frequency alongside gross margin, pricing decisions will look cleaner on paper than they behave in cash generation.
