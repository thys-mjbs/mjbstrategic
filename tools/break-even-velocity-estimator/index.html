Break-Even Velocity Estimator

A business adds fixed costs when it hires, expands, or commits to new capacity. This diagnostic measures the revenue growth required to keep the cost base economically justified.

In real operations, new fixed costs usually arrive before the extra orders are stable. The gap shows up as compressed cash generation, weaker coverage, and pressure to discount.

This matters because expansion should be paced to the rate the business can actually convert demand into margin. If revenue velocity is unrealistic, the decision becomes a liquidity and execution risk.

Use average monthly revenue from recent trading periods.

Include rent, salaries, software, and other fixed overheads.

Enter incremental monthly costs from the planned expansion.

Use gross margin after direct costs, before overhead.

This tool assumes you have basic internal visibility into monthly revenue, gross margin, fixed costs, and order throughput. If these numbers are not tracked consistently, the real risk is often not the calculation but the lack of operational reporting discipline required to manage expansion.
